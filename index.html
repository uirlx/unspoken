Can Audio Language Models Listen Between the Lines?\n A Study on Metaphorical Reasoning via UNSPOKEN

Abstract. While recent Audio Language Models (ALMs) have achieved notable progress in voice interaction and audio analysis, their capabilities for deep metaphorical reasoning remains underexplored, especially with respect to audio-specific nuances. To bridge this gap, we introduce UNSPOKEN, a bilingual question answering benchmark specifically designed for an in-depth assessment of ALMs' metaphor comprehension capabilities. Unlike text-based benchmarks, UNSPOKEN emphasizes audio-specific cues that are crucial for metaphor comprehension yet often absent in transcriptions, such as prosody, phonetic ambiguity, and emotional inflection. Extensive experiments with 22 models show that even the best-performing model achieves only 69.5% accuracy, which still lags significantly behind the human average of 81.1%. This highlights a persistent gap between current models and human-level performance in metaphorical reasoning. Delving deeper into the error patterns, we identify five key failure modes that illuminate the inherent limitations of current ALMs in reasoning, context, prosody, and cultural awareness. We make UNSPOKEN publicly available and hope it will serve as a crucial resource for driving ALMs toward deeper audio understanding.

Ethics Statement

Given the ability of DialogueAgents to synthesize speech while preserving the speaker's identity, potential risks of misuse, such as deceiving voice recognition systems or impersonating specific individuals, may arise. In our experiments, we operate under the assumption that users willingly agree to be the designated speaker for speech synthesis. In the event of the model's application to unknown speakers in real-world scenarios, it is imperative to establish a protocol ensuring explicit consent from speakers for the utilization of their voices. Additionally, implementing a synthetic speech detection model is recommended to mitigate the potential for misuse.
